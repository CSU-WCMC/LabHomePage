WCMC Lab conducts research at the intersection of wireless communication, edge computing, Internet of Things, intelligent vehicular networks, distributed systems, big data analytics, and trustworthy intelligent computing. Our work combines theoretical modeling, algorithm design, system optimization, and engineering practice for networked intelligent systems.

The following summary is based on Prof. Xiaoheng Deng's public research project list and related lab profile information.

## Research Directions

### Wireless Networks and Mobile Computing

We study next-generation wireless networks, mobile computing systems, and network resource optimization. Representative topics include wireless network protocol optimization, congestion control, mobile information systems, and end-to-end quality-of-service modeling.

### Edge Computing and Cloud-Edge-Device Collaboration

We work on resource management, task offloading, service deployment, and collaborative optimization in edge computing environments. Recent projects emphasize cloud-edge-device collaboration, edge intelligence, security, privacy protection, and trusted resource management.

### Intelligent Vehicular Networks and Transportation Systems

The lab investigates vehicle-road collaboration, software-defined vehicular networks, radar-video fusion, roadside perception, connected vehicles, and intelligent transportation applications. These projects connect wireless networking, sensing, edge computing, and data-driven optimization.

### IoT, Big Data, and Cyber-Physical Applications

Our project portfolio includes IoT platforms, big data analytics, industrial equipment health management, smart city planning, medical and clinical data platforms, and information system design for real-world applications.

### Distributed Systems, Trust, and Network Security

Earlier and ongoing work covers distributed computing, grid computing, P2P trust optimization, online social network modeling, secure audit systems, and privacy-enhancing collaborative computing.

## Project Portfolio

The team has led or participated in projects funded by national, provincial, local, industry, and institutional sources, including:

- National Natural Science Foundation of China projects, including key/enterprise joint fund projects and general projects.
- Hunan provincial key R&D programs, natural science foundation projects, science and technology plans, and transportation science and technology programs.
- Ministry of Education doctoral fund and teaching reform projects.
- China Postdoctoral Science Foundation and Hunan postdoctoral support programs.
- Open projects from national key laboratories.
- Industry and local-government collaborations with telecommunications, transportation, smart city, cybersecurity, industrial equipment, and healthcare information systems.

## Application Domains

The research portfolio spans both fundamental methods and deployed application scenarios:

- Intelligent transportation and vehicle-road collaboration.
- Space-air-ground and vehicular edge computing networks.
- Industrial equipment health management and fault analysis.
- Smart city planning and local government information systems.
- Clinical big data platforms and healthcare information systems.
- Network security, privacy protection, and trusted computing platforms.
