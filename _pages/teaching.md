# Join WCMC Lab

The Wireless Communication and Intelligent Computing Laboratory (WCMC Lab) at the School of Electronic Information, Central South University welcomes motivated students and collaborators who are interested in networked systems, intelligent computing, and real-world applications.

The lab is led by **Prof. Xiaoheng Deng**. We work on research problems at the intersection of wireless communication, edge computing, Internet of Things, intelligent vehicular networks, distributed systems, network security, artificial intelligence, large language models, and edge intelligence.

## Who We Welcome

We welcome inquiries from:

- Prospective PhD students.
- Prospective master's students.
- Undergraduate students interested in research training, course projects, graduation projects, or longer-term research involvement.
- Visiting students, research interns, and collaborators from universities, research institutes, and industry.

Admission, enrollment, funding, and visiting arrangements follow the relevant policies and procedures of Central South University, the School of Electronic Information, and applicable programs. Please refer to official university and school announcements for current requirements and deadlines.

## Research Directions

Possible research topics include, but are not limited to:

- Wireless communication and next-generation networked systems.
- Edge computing, mobile computing, and distributed systems.
- Internet of Things, sensing systems, and cyber-physical systems.
- Intelligent vehicular networks, connected vehicles, and intelligent transportation systems.
- Network security, privacy protection, and trustworthy networked systems.
- AI for networking, data-driven network optimization, and intelligent resource management.
- Large language models, edge intelligence, and AI-enabled systems.

Specific projects may vary from year to year depending on ongoing research programs, student background, and collaboration needs.

## Candidate Background

Applicants with one or more of the following backgrounds are encouraged to get in touch:

- Communication engineering, electronic information, information and communication engineering, electronic science and technology, computer science, artificial intelligence, automation, software engineering, data science, cybersecurity, or related areas.
- Solid foundations in mathematics, algorithms, computer networks, wireless communication, distributed systems, machine learning, or systems engineering.
- Programming and engineering experience in languages or tools such as Python, C/C++, MATLAB, Linux, networking tools, simulation platforms, embedded/IoT platforms, or machine learning frameworks.
- Research experience in papers, patents, competitions, open-source projects, system prototypes, or engineering projects.
- Good reading and writing ability in English and a willingness to work on open-ended research problems.

No single background is required for every topic. We value curiosity, reliability, clear communication, and the ability to learn independently.

## Application Materials

When contacting us, please include the following materials if available:

- A brief email describing your research interests and the type of position or collaboration you are seeking.
- Curriculum vitae or resume.
- Academic transcripts.
- Representative publications, preprints, technical reports, patents, project descriptions, code repositories, or other relevant work.
- For prospective graduate students: information about your intended degree program and admission timeline, if known.
- For visiting students or collaborators: proposed visiting period, research topic, and possible funding or program arrangement, if applicable.

Materials may be sent in English or Chinese.
