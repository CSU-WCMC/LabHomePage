**WCMC Lab** is the Wireless Communication and Intelligent Computing Laboratory at Central South University. The lab is led by **Prof. Xiaoheng Deng**, Dean of the School of Electronic Information, doctoral supervisor, and Hunan Province "Furong Scholar" Distinguished Professor.

Prof. Deng also serves as Director of the Hunan Engineering Research Center for Deep Sensing and Intelligent Communication, Director of the Hunan Engineering Center for Data Sensing and Exchange Equipment, Vice President of the Hunan Electronics Society, Standing Director of the Hunan Computer Federation, CCF Distinguished Member, and IEEE Senior Member.

Our research focuses on next-generation networked systems and intelligent computing. Current directions include wireless networks, edge computing, Internet of Things, big data, intelligent vehicular networks, software-defined networking, distributed systems, online social networks, and related applications in intelligent transportation and cyber-physical systems.

The team has long worked on both theory and engineering practice. According to Prof. Deng's official profile, he has led or participated in more than 30 research projects, published more than 150 papers in journals and conferences including IEEE TON, TMC, TDSC, TKDE, TITS, TVT, IoT, TNSE, CVPR, ICASSP, ICC, GLOBECOM, WCNC, ICTAI, and IPCCC, and has been granted 34 invention patents, published 2 books, and received 2 provincial or ministerial science and technology awards.

We welcome students and collaborators interested in wireless communication, mobile computing, edge intelligence, IoT systems, artificial intelligence, big data, and data-driven network optimization. The lab recruits PhD and master's students from information and communication engineering, intelligent science and technology, electronic information, electronic science and technology, artificial intelligence, big data, network and information security, and communication engineering.
